The Problem

Private clinics lose money on billing errors they cannot see

Σφάλματα χρέωσης που περνούν απαρατήρητα

Every private clinic in Greece processes hundreds of invoices per month — to EOPYY, to private insurers, and directly to patients. Hidden inside that volume are systematic errors that go undetected because no one has time to check them manually.

  • → Duplicate invoices — the same procedure billed twice to the same insurer, often months apart. Insurers eventually detect these and claw back payments with penalties.
  • → Upcoding — a procedure billed above its allowed maximum rate under EOPYY or insurer tariffs. A common audit trigger that puts EOPYY contracts at risk.
  • → Phantom billing — an invoice raised for a date when the patient has no recorded appointment. Can indicate data entry error or, in an audit, look like fraud.
  • → Unbundling — procedure components that should be billed as one bundled code are billed individually at a higher combined total.

These are not hypothetical. In a dataset of 506 invoices modelled on private clinic billing data, the HealthLedgerAI prototype detected 70 alerts across 8 error types.

The Solution

HealthLedgerAI

Automated billing compliance detection — in seconds, not weeks

HealthLedgerAI is a Python-based prototype that reads a clinic's billing data (invoices, appointments, procedure rates, insurer networks) and automatically flags billing anomalies — before they reach an insurer, before an audit, and before a payment is made that will need to be reversed.

Relevance to Embryolab

Why fertility clinics are a natural fit

Γιατί οι κλινικές γονιμότητας έχουν ιδιαίτερα σύνθετη χρέωση

Fertility clinics operate in one of the most billing-intensive environments in private healthcare. A single IVF cycle generates multiple billing events across different categories — consultations, laboratory procedures, medications, anaesthesia, embryology services — each with different tariff rules.

  • → EOPYY + private insurers simultaneously — IVF is covered by EOPYY up to 3 cycles for women under 50. This means clinics bill two different systems with different rules for the same patient. Mixed billing increases the risk of errors.
  • → High procedure values — IVF cycle costs range from €3,000–€6,000. A single upcoding or duplicate billing error at this scale represents significant financial exposure.
  • → Multi-step billing sequences — each cycle involves 8–12 separate billing events. Date mismatches and duplicate entries are common in high-volume, multi-step billing processes.
  • → ISO certification environment — Embryolab is ISO 9001:2015 and EN 15224 certified, meaning it already operates to documented process standards. A billing compliance tool fits naturally into that framework.
