Strategic Window

Why HealthLedgerAI — Why Now

EIMC has completed two major investments in 18 months: €5M for four new operating rooms (Feb 2023) and €40M for a brand new 12,000 m² International Oncology Center (2024). JCI accreditation was achieved in December 2025. The hospital is now in revenue ramp-up mode — new clinical services are live but the billing complexity has multiplied. Group working capital turned negative (−€20.5M) due to the investment surge. The optimal window is now: complex new services need sophisticated billing, the CFO needs revenue optimization, and JCI requires continuous compliance audit trails. HealthLedgerAI addresses all three simultaneously.

Working Capital Pressure — Direct Revenue Case

Athens Medical Group reported negative consolidated working capital of −€20.5M (June 2024) and took a €5M revolving credit facility from the National Bank of Greece. Net profit fell 77% in FY2024 and 84% in H1 2025. Faster EOPYY collection, reduced insurance claim rejections, and oncology billing optimisation translate directly into working capital improvement — a CFO argument with a number attached.

Pain Points → HealthLedgerAI Fit
01

Dual-Payer Billing at Scale

EIMC absorbs EOPYY co-payments up to €1,000 per stay when patients use both EOPYY and private insurance. Reconciling two payer systems per case, across 10+ private insurers with different fee schedules, is massive manual overhead.

→ EOPYY + Insurance Reconciliation Engine

02

Oncology Center Billing Ramp-Up

The €40M Oncology Center opened in 2024 with 120 new chemo beds, three linear accelerators, IMRT/IGRT/VMAT protocols, and PET/CT. These are among the most complex billing scenarios in all of healthcare — wrong coding means significant revenue loss.

→ Oncology Protocol Billing AI · EOPYY Rate Validation

03

Working Capital Pressure

Consolidated working capital hit −€20.5M in June 2024. Net profit fell 77% in FY2024. Faster insurance claim collection and reduced EOPYY rejection rates translate directly into cash — a quantifiable ROI argument for the CFO.

→ Claim Pre-Validation · Rejection Rate Reduction

Cross-Border Patient Billing

7,000 foreign patients/year from 53 countries, with AMG diagnostic referral centers in Sofia, Belgrade, Bucharest, and Skopje. EU cross-border directive billing, bilateral health agreement invoicing, and direct international self-pay all require separate billing pathways.

JCI accreditation (December 2025) requires ongoing monitoring of 1,000+ measurable elements. Documentation, audit trails, and quality management must be maintained continuously — not just at the accreditation review cycle.

→ JCI Audit Trail · Compliance Documentation Engine
